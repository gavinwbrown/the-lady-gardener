Tracey the Gardengal
Welcome

Did you know that the name for a lady who gardens is either a gardenerette or a gardengal, hence the name of my website. Here are some pictures taken on my iPhone of some of my favourite plants and flowers. Whilst by no means an exhaustive list I hope this will inspire you to maybe try a couple of these plants in your own garden, if you haven't already.

Pieris

Pieris

An attractive evergreen shrub, grown for its handsome foliage and display of early spring flowers. The young leaves are often bronze or red, maturing to dark green. They contrast beautifully with the small, white jewel-like flowers, and bees love them.

Forsythia

Forsythia

Flowers in early spring with lovely bright yellow flowers. A very hardy shrub that is easy to grow as it likes a variety of different soil types. Grow in full sun or light shade. Plant in autumn or winter for a burst of spring colour.

Rhododendron

Rhododendron

A great plant for a splash of spring colour. Really likes lots of rain and light shade.

Azalea

Rhododendron

Once thought to be entirely separate to rhododendrons azaleas are now considered to be part of the rhododendron family. Whilst they share many of the same characteristics there are plenty of differences too. Make sure that your dog doesn't eat them though as they are poisonous to our furry friends.

Dianthus

Dianthus

Known as garden pinks because their leaves look like they've been trimmed with pinking shears. Hardy perennials that flower through most of the summer, great in borders but also in containers. Easy to maintain making them a great choice for a garden.

Hyacinth

Hyacinthus

Perfect for borders or containers. if in containers make sure they're raised with pot feet. They prefer well dained fertile soil and a sunny position in the garden. Very fragrant.

Tulip

Tulipa

A perrenial that will bring some lovely colour to a spring garden.

Magnolia

Magnolia

Lovely shrubs and trees that can be grown in pots. Some are fragranced and colours range from white through pink to magenta and even yellow as in this picture. Most flower in spring but there are some that flower in summer.

Cosmos

Cosmos

Cosmos are sun-loving plants with a long flowering season that runs well into autumn. They fill borders with a profusion of flowers and colours, contrasted with masses of feathery foliage.

Buddleia

Buddleja

Also known as summer lilac or butterfly-bush they are fast growing shrubs that love the Sun and thrive in well drained soil. Insects love them because of how much nectar they produce. Flowers for 4-6 weeks in late summer.

Rose

Rosa

Roses are a classic and instantly recognisable plant, many are fragrant, ideal for almost every style of garden. They flower abundantly from early summer in a range of colours. Many flower all summer long.

Osteospermum

Osteospermum

Osteospermum, otherwise known as the Cape or African daisy, is a brilliant choice for long-lasting, colourful blooms through the summer and into autumn. There are many colours to choose from and the vibrant daisy flowers are a firm favourite of bees and other pollinators.

Sunflower

Helianthus

Sunflowers are iconic and as one of the easiest flowers to grow they thrive in full sun and aren’t picky about soil. You’re probably most familiar with these giant types that grow very tall and have oversized yellow flowers. But there are many other types of sunflowers too.

Osteospermum

Osteospermum

Included again because of the incredibly beautiful flower, it looks almost alien.

Opium poppy

Papaver somniferum

An attractive plant with blue-green foliage from which extravagant blooms emerge in summer. The flowers can be single or double, in a variety of colours. Individual flowers don't last long but others follow, each producing a large seed head.

Astilbe

Astilbe

Also known as false goat's beard these plants are an easy to grow hardy perennial. They thrive in damp soil and can be grown in containers. Flower from summer through into autumn.

Lavender

Lavandula

Loved by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Produce a mass of flowers and are very fragrant. They grow best in a sunny border with well drained soil but also do very well in containers.

Achillea

Achillea

Also known as Yarrows they will grow in a wide variety of soils but don't let them get waterlogged. Enjoy full sun to partial shade. Are loved by aphids though so keep an eye out for those little pests.

California Poppy

Eschscholzia californica

Also known as California sunlight or cup of gold. Need well drained soil and plenty of Sun to flower. Don't produce nectar but lots of pollen so again a firm favourite with bees.

Echinacea

Echinacea

Also known as coneflower these plants are again very popular with pollinators and have very distinctive flowers that bring a loevly splash of colour to any garden. The daisy like flowers appear in late summer.

Agapanthus

Agapanthus

Also known as Peter Pan lily or Lily of the Nile. Flower from mid-summer through into autumn. Can be grown in containers but also look great in borders. They love the Sun and colours vary including blue, purple and sometimes even white.

Viola

Viola

An easy to grow plant that rewards regular deadheading with a long period of flowering. A good container plant but will need regular watering if grown this way. They don't mind partial shade either.

Pyracantha

Pyracantha

A very pretty evergreen shrub also known as firethorn. Grows flowers and berries which can be red, yellow or orange. loved by pollinators and birds love the berries. Easy to grow in most soils making this plant a great choice for most gardens.

Acer

Acer

Also known as the Japanese maple they are actually tress and are ideal to grow in containers. Slow growing and perfect for a smaller garden, creating a splash of colour wherever they are.

Hellebore

Helleborus

A classic winter flowering plant but longlasting so you'll still see them in spring. They like moist soil and light shade. Also known as Christmas or Lenten roses they aren't roses but actually belong to the buttercup family.

Crocus

Crocus

If you want a splash of colour through the winter and into spring then the crocus is a must have. Great in borders, containers or even in lawns. They are generally hardy, enjoy the sun and like well drained soil.
