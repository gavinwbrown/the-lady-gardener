Here are some pictures taken on my iPhone of some of my favourite plants and flowers and whilst by no means an exhaustive list I hope this will inspire you to maybe try a couple of these plants in your own Garden , if you haven't already.

Cosmos

Cosmos are sun-loving plants with a long flowering season that runs well into autumn. They fill borders with a profusion of flowers and colours, contrasted with masses of feathery foliage.

Rose

Roses are a classic and instantly recognisable plant, many are fragrant, ideal for almost every style of garden. They flower abundantly from early summer in a range of colours. Many flower all summer long.

Pieres

An attractive evergreen shrub, grown for its handsome foliage and display of early spring flowers. The young leaves are often bronze or red, maturing to dark green. They contrast beautifully with the small, white jewel-like flowers, and bees love them.

Osteospernum

Osteospermum, otherwise known as the Cape or African daisy, is a brilliant choice for long-lasting, colourful blooms through the summer and into autumn. There are many colours to choose from and the vibrant daisy flowers are a firm favourite of bees and other pollinators.

Sunflower

Sunflowers are iconic and as one of the easiest flowers to grow they thrive in full sun and aren’t picky about soil. You’re probably most familiar with these giant types that grow very tall and have oversized yellow flowers. But there are many other types of sunflowers too.

Opium poppy

An attractive plant with blue-green foliage from which extravagant blooms emerge in summer. The flowers can be single or double, in a variety of colours. Individual flowers don't last long but others follow, each producing a large seed head.
