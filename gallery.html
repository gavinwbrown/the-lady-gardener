Achillea or Yarrow

Traditional border perennials. They have long been valued for their feathery foliage and the beautiful, flat-topped flowers that bloom right through the summer months.

Cosmos

Cosmos are sun-loving plants with a long flowering season that runs well into autumn. They fill borders with a profusion of flowers and colours, contrasted with masses of feathery foliage.

Marguerite Daisy

A tender evergreen shrub that will provide masses of colour from spring to early autumn. One of the best loved of all the summer bedding plants due to the long-lasting flowers, the pure white daisies with yellow centres look great above the rich green foliage. It needs a sunny spot for best results

Rose

Roses are a classic and instantly recognisable plant, many are fragrant, ideal for almost every style of garden. They flower abundantly from early summer in a range of colours. Many flower all summer long.

Geranium or Pelargonium

Popular bedding plants, providing a burst of colour and fragrance throughout summer. They're easy to grow and look great in pots. They work well planted on their own or combined with other plants such as lavender and nemesia.

Snowdrops

A sign that spring has sprung, these little bulbs are among the first plants to flower in the new year. Snowdrops will grow in most soil types, apart from permanently wet soil, preferring partial shade.

Primrose

Like Snowdrops Primroses are a sure sign of spring. They are an important nectar source for butterflies. Suitable for use in garden beds and borders as well as in containers.

Pieres

An attractive evergreen shrub, grown for its handsome foliage and display of early spring flowers. The young leaves are often bronze or red, maturing to dark green. They contrast beautifully with the small, white jewel-like flowers, and bees love them.

Osteospernum

Osteospermum, otherwise known as the Cape or African daisy, is a brilliant choice for long-lasting, colourful blooms through the summer and into autumn. There are many colours to choose from and the vibrant daisy flowers are another favourite of bees and other pollinators.

Sunflower

Sunflowers are iconic, easy-to-grow plants. One of the easiest flowers to grow, they thrive in full sun and aren’t picky about soil. You’re probably most familiar with these giant types that grow very tall and have oversized yellow flowers. But there are many other types of sunflowers too.

Crocus

Flowering mainly in late winter to early spring, crocuses look great in borders, containers or planted in lawns to create lovely splashes of colour.

Opium poppy

An attractive plant with blue-green foliage from which extravagant blooms emerge in summer. The flowers can be single or double, in a variety of colours Individual flowers don't last long but others follow, each producing a large seed head.
